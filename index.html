The Challenge

vivo was the Official Smartphone of FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022 — a global sponsorship that created a rare opportunity for the Uzbekistan market. The challenge was translating a premium global IP into a locally resonant, multi-month campaign that drove measurable sell-out across a portfolio of 6 smartphone models, through both open market and key account retail channels simultaneously.

The Approach

Rather than a single burst campaign, I planned a phased 5-month promotional arc running August through December. The campaign opened with a high-stakes grand prize mechanic — 10 trips to the FIFA Final in Doha including flights and hotel — to generate registrations and awareness, then transitioned to gifting-based promotions to maintain momentum through the tournament. Each channel received a tailored execution: co-marketing activations for key accounts and in-store promo points with trained promoters across open market locations.

The Execution

Campaign creative ran bilingual in Uzbek and Russian. Customers registered through a dedicated digital platform providing traceable participation data. Winners were announced via Instagram Live — extending reach beyond the promotion window. I coordinated partner briefings, campaign materials, timelines, and execution across Texnomart, Elmakon, MediaPark, and Zoodmall simultaneously.

The Brief

GreenEco is a hypothetical bio-based and biodegradable products company targeting businesses and consumers in Uzbekistan committed to sustainable living. The assignment was to develop a comprehensive two-year digital marketing plan — from situation analysis and strategy through to budget allocation and KPI framework.

The Strategy

I applied the RACE framework (Reach, Act, Convert, Engage) to structure a full-funnel digital strategy. The Reach phase focused on SEO-optimised website development, social media presence, and sustainability influencer partnerships. The Act phase introduced lead capture, free trials, and expanded distribution. Convert focused on A/B testing, retargeting campaigns, and conversion optimisation. Engage built long-term loyalty through referral programmes, events, and community content.

The Plan

The Year 1 budget of $81,200 was allocated across content marketing (18%), CPC/CPM advertising (13%), social media (15%), SEO (8%), branding (12%), paid search (12%), and promotions (12%). The plan included a 2-year implementation timeline with quarterly milestones and measurable KPIs for brand awareness, lead generation, and market positioning.

Year 1 Budget Allocation
Content marketing and social media received the highest allocation — reflecting a brand-building first strategy appropriate for a new market entrant.

The Idea

Writing campaign briefs takes marketers 2–4 hours — even though the structure is largely the same every time. I wanted to build a tool that generates a complete, professional campaign brief in seconds, based on a few guided inputs. The twist: I had zero coding experience going in.

What I Built

A 4-step guided web app that collects campaign objectives, audience details, channels, and budget — then calls the Claude API to generate a tailored brief instantly. Built using Claude for code generation, Netlify for serverless functions, and the Gemini API for the AI layer. Deployed live in one day.

What It Proved

This project was never just about the tool. It demonstrated that a marketer who understands how to frame a problem, structure a prompt, and debug a workflow can ship real software without writing a single line of code manually. That is a different kind of skill — and an increasingly valuable one.
